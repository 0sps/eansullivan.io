Hyperbole – Exaggeration to make something stand out

Oxymoron – Contradictory phrase (Jumbo Shrimp)

Cliché – An overused phrase

Allusion – A reference to something in the past

Symbol – An object that stands for an idea

Consonance – Repetition of consonant sounds at the end of words

Alliteration - The repetition of consonant sounds at the beginning of a series of words (Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers.)

Assonance - The repetition of vowel sounds in a series of words (Why fly by night?)

Onomatopoeia - Words that sound like what they mean (Splash, Pop, Clink)

Simile - A comparison using the words “like” or “as” (Mr. Daniel shows an elongated photo of him and his wife as an example)

Metaphor - A comparison that does NOT use "like" or "as". (I would put his example but they annoy me so I'm done)

Personification - Giving human traits or characteristics to non-human things

Aphorism - a short, wise saying (“To be great is to be misunderstood.” - Emerson. “A penny saved is a penny earned.” – Ben Franklin.)

Anaphora - Literary term; starting every line with the same word or phrase for effect.

Apostrophe – addressing someone not present or non-human. (“Death, you may come and take me now…”

Elegy – a long poem memorializing the death of someone

Enjambment - (in verse) the continuation of a sentence without a pause beyond the end of a line, couplet, or stanza. Usually these lines do not have a punctuation mark at the end; it is a running on of a thought from one line to another without final punctuation.

Stanza – a group of lines in a poem (quatrain, octave, etc.)

Sonnet – a 14-line poem with a fixed rhyme scheme

Allusion – a reference to another work of art or lit (“Is this a cross I have to bear?”)

Idiom - The thing we did the Word Wizard for

1) The chief contribution of Lord Byron to English Literature is the “Byronic Hero”. Identify the characteristics of the Byronic Hero and then choose a character we’ve read about THIS SEMESTER that could be considered a Byronic Hero. Justify your choice with examples from the reading.

 

2) Discuss the use of symbolism in 1984. Be sure to mention specific symbols and what they represent. You should also discuss examples from the text that prove that a particular symbol represents a particular idea. You should discuss a minimum of THREE symbols.

 

3) Contrast the Neo-classical and the Romantic Periods of literature.

 

4) Discuss specific examples of irony from The Tenth Man. Also, be sure to identify the types of irony used (verbal, dramatic, and/or situations)

 

5) Is Life of Pi a Romantic work? Why or why not?

 

6) What lessons does Life of Pi tell us about fiction? And how can the book claim to make us believe in God?

 

7) Choose a comedic film, play, or story and explain the comedic tools it uses.

 

 

Make sure your essays have identifiable thesis statements. Each answer should be 200-300 words. Be aggressive and strong in development. Proper grammar, spelling, and punctuation are expected. Be sure to cite specific examples from the reading(s) to support your claims.
